> About

I am a candidate for BS in Mathematics at Carnegie Mellon University. On campus, I am a teaching assistant for CMU's 15-122: Principles of Imperative Computation course, and work as an AI research assistant for the Mohimani Lab.


I am interested in the intersection of mathematics and computer science, and am currently exploring the fields of machine learning. Specifically, I am working at Mohimani Lab to help use machine learning models for predicting the structures of non-ribosomal peptide synthetases (NRPSs) from their amino acid sequences. NRPSs are large, modular enzymes that produce a wide variety of natural products, including antibiotics, antifungals, and even anticancer drugs.

AdenPredictor

Python, Rust

Working as part of Mohimani Lab on improving the accuracy of a machine learning model that predicts the Adenylation (A) domain of a non-ribosomal peptide synthetase (NRPS) given its amino acid sequence. A-domains are responsible for selecting and activating amino acids for incorporation into the peptide product. I am working on parsing the MIBiG database of NRPSs genes and analyzing the data to determine local and global contexts that help predict the A-domain specificity. For example, an A-domain preceded by a heterocyclization (Cy) domain is more likely to select Serine (Ser) as its amino acid substrate.

RNAPredictor

Python, Pandas, Tensorflow

Worked with a team to develop a deep learning framework for RNA secondary structure prediction by calculating structure probabilities and then maximizing probability using dynamic programming. Wrote the DP recurrence relations and helped clean dataset for the Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model. Compared deep learning model performance against existing Nussinov and Zuker algorithms on 1000 sequences from bprna dataset. Results showed that our framework outperformed the Nussinov algorithm, but not the Zucker algorithm. We concluded this is likely because our model was not trained on pseudo-knotted sequences, which the Zuker algorithm is able to handle.
